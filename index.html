Resource Transparency Platform - Prototype

*You are currently exploring a beta version of the Resource Transparency Platform tool. Please note that the tool is under development and the results provided are for demonstration purposes only.

The Resource Transparency Platform offers users a one-stop, searchable database of information on country-level public, and private financial flows across the plastics lifecycle to address plastic pollution.

  • Plastic Pollution Financial Flow information is available by year, geography, sources, applications, and types of financial flows that took place between January 1, 2018 and December 31, 2023.
  • Track the direction and distribution of financial flows to mobilize, and direct capital to high-impact pathways.
  • Gain tailored insights to your queries through the AI-powered search feature.

For example, the tool can be used to track the amount of development finance committed to Africa since 2018 and the share of the commitments that have been disbursed.

Access Tool

An estimated US$ 15.4 trillion in private sector investments and US$ 1.5 trillion in public expenditure are needed between 2025 and 2040 to reduce annual mismanaged plastic volumes by 90% relative to 2019 levels.

There is limited transparency around the deployment of capital, the financing gaps that exist, and of capital mobilization or redirection needed to solve the plastic pollution challenge. Data on finance that is available for tackling plastic pollution is a critical input to ensure that sufficient capital is mobilized from different sources and directed towards relevant geographies and solutions.

To address this knowledge gap, The Circulate Initiative plans to develop a Resource Transparency Platform to provide a transparent view of all Plastic Pollution Financial Flows. Plastic pollution financial flows refers to financing targeted towards addressing plastic pollution through circular, non-circular, or other supporting pathways.

With more than 40 data fields captured across domestic finance, development finance, the financial mechanism (subject to confirmation), and private finance, the Resource Transparency platform aims to support decision making through:

  • Improved availability of consolidated financial data for national action plans, needs assessments, and targeted funding calls.
  • Visibility into existing financial flows, and where additional private investments can be complementary.
  • Insights on the impact of existing policies and evidence- base for new financial policy-making

The Resource Transparency Platform builds on The Circulate Initiative’s expertise in developing the Plastics Circularity Investment Tracker, the world’s first tool to track the scale of private investments driving a circular economy for plastics globally

Scope of the Platform

Year

Data provided in the platform is for the period of January 1, 2018 to December 31, 2023.

Geography

The platform consolidates information on public and private financial flows that have taken place globally.

Sources of Finance

The sources of finance captured in the platform include both Public and Private sources.

  • Public Finance
    • Domestic Finance
    • Development Finance
    • Multilateral Environmental Agreement Finance
  • Private Finance

Who can use this Platform

Governments

Member states can use the tool as a go-to resource for monitoring financial flows to make informed financial decisions that align with national priorities

Multilateral Institutions

Multilateral institutions, including development banks, can analyze financial flows to guide the distribution of development assistance packages, such as grants and other concessional funding.

Financial institutions

Banks and other financial institutions can assess the regions and solutions that have received investment, which can support their investment and loan allocation decisions.

Corporates

Corporates can identify investment trends and gaps in the value chain to inform their business strategies.
